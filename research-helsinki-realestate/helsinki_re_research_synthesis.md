**Calculation basis:** The tax is calculated on the **kauppahinta (selling price)** only. Yhtiölaina (housing company loan) is **not** included in the tax base. This is significant for Category B listings (low myyntihinta + heavy yhtiölaina): the buyer pays 1.5% only on the low selling price, not on the velaton hinta.

**Example for our budget:**
- Standard purchase at €100,000 (no yhtiölaina): varainsiirtovero = €100,000 × 1.5% = **€1,500**

**Payment deadline:** Must be declared and paid within 2 months of purchase. Declared via OmaVero. No first-time buyer exemption exists since 2024 (source: https://www.vero.fi/henkiloasiakkaat/omaisuus/varainsiirtovero/).

## Section 7: Tax Treatment of Rental Income & Capital Gains

### 7.1 Rental Income Taxation (Vuokratulon verotus)

Rental income is classified as **pääomatuloa** (capital income) and taxed at the following rates (source: https://www.vero.fi/henkiloasiakkaat/omaisuus/vuokratulot/kuinka-paljon-vuokratuloista-menee-veroa/):

| Capital Income Band | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to €30,000 | **30%** |
| Over €30,000 | **34%** |

Tax is calculated on **net rental income** = gross rent − deductible expenses.

### 7.2 Deductible Expenses from Rental Income (Asunto-osake)

The following expenses can be deducted from rental income (source: https://www.vero.fi/henkiloasiakkaat/omaisuus/vuokratulot/vahennykset):

**Directly deductible in full (year of payment):**

| Expense | Deductibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Hoitovastike | 100% | Main deduction for most investors |
| Vesimaksut | 100% | Water charges paid to taloyhtiö |
| Vuokranvälittäjä palkkio | 100% | Tenant acquisition costs, ads |
| Vuokranantajan vakuutus | 100% | Landlord's insurance premiums |
| Matkakulut | 0.27 €/km (2025) | To viewings, inspections, yhtiökokous |
| Pääomavastike (rahoitusvastike) | 100% IF tuloutettu | Only if tuloutetttu in taloyhtiö kirjanpito; if rahastoitu → not deductible from rent but adds to hankintameno at sale |

**Renovation costs (remontti):**

| Type | Deduction Method | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Vuosikorjaus** (annual repair) | 100% in year of payment | Painting, appliance replacement, fixtures |
| **Perusparannus** (improvement) | 10% per year (tasapoisto) over 10 years | New bathroom, structural improvements |
| **Irtain <€1,200** or <3yr life | 100% in year of purchase | Small furniture, appliances |
| **Irtain >€1,200** and >3yr life | 25% annual depreciation (menojäännöspoisto) | Expensive appliances |

**Critical note on pääomavastike/rahoitusvastike:** The deductibility depends on the taloyhtiö's accounting treatment:
- **Tuloutettu** (income-stated) → deductible from rental income annually
- **Rahastoitu** (capitalized/funded) → NOT deductible from rent, but added to hankintameno (acquisition cost) and deducted at sale when calculating capital gains

The investor should always verify the accounting treatment with the isännöitsijä before purchase.

### 7.4 Capital Gains Tax (Luovutusvoittovero) — Sale of Investment Property

When selling a property that was NOT your own residence, capital gains are taxed as pääomatulo at 30%/34% (source: https://www.vero.fi/henkiloasiakkaat/omaisuus/asunnon-myynti/oman-asunnon-myynti/):

**Calculation:**
```
Luovutusvoitto = Myyntihinta − (Ostohinta + Varainsiirtovero + Hankkimiskulut)
```

**OR** the taxpayer may use the **hankintameno-olettama** (acquisition cost presumption):
- Ownership **<10 years**: 20% of myyntihinta
- Ownership **≥10 years**: 40% of myyntihinta

The taxpayer chooses whichever method produces the lower taxable gain. The hankintameno-olettama and actual costs **cannot be combined**.

**Own-residence exemption (oman asunnon verovapaus):**
The sale is **completely tax-free** if BOTH conditions are met:
1. The seller has owned the property for at least **2 years**
2. The seller OR a family member (spouse, minor child) has lived there as their **permanent home continuously for at least 2 years** during the ownership period

**Strategic implication for this investment:**
- During the rental period (5–10 years): the flat is an investment property → capital gains taxable
- **If the child moves in and lives there continuously for 2+ years**, then sells → the sale qualifies for the own-residence exemption → **tax-free**
- This is the optimal exit strategy: rent for 5–10 years, child moves in for university/work, lives there 2+ years, then sells tax-free

**Capital gains tax example (if selling as investment property):**
| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Purchase price (2026) | €90,000 |
| Varainsiirtovero paid | €1,350 |
| Sale price (2033, after 7 years) | €100,000 |
| Actual gain: €100,000 − €91,350 = | €8,650 |
| Hankintameno-olettama (20%): €100,000 × 0.20 = €20,000 → gain = | €80,000 |
| **Use actual costs** (lower tax) → taxable gain | **€8,650** |
| Tax at 30% | **€2,595** |

**Capital loss:** If the property is sold at a loss, the loss is deductible from capital income (luovutusvoitot first, then other pääomatulot). Losses carry forward for 5 years. Losses from own-residence sales that would have been tax-free are NOT deductible.

## Section 5: Yhtiölaina Strategy & Risks

### 5.1 How Yhtiölaina Works

Yhtiölaina is a loan taken by the taloyhtiö (housing company) and allocated to each apartment based on shares or square meters. When an apartment is sold, the buyer takes over the remaining yhtiölaina for that unit. The **myyntihinta** (selling price) is the cash paid by the buyer, while the **velaton hinta** (debt-free price) = myyntihinta + remaining yhtiölaina (source: https://asuntosalkunrakentaja.fi/asuntosijoittaminen/rahoitusvastike-ja-yhtiolaina/).

**Rahoitusvastike** (financing charge) is the monthly payment to repay the yhtiölaina. It is NOT an expense — it is a loan repayment that builds equity.

### 5.2 Tax Treatment of Rahoitusvastike

| Accounting Treatment | Tax Deductibility | Effect at Sale |
|---|---|---|
| **Tuloutettu** (income-stated) | **Fully deductible** from rental income each year | Reduces hankintameno at sale → higher taxable gain |
| **Rahastoitu** (capitalized) | NOT deductible from rental income | Added to hankintameno → reduces taxable gain at sale |

In new builds (uudiskohteet), rahoitusvastike is virtually always tuloutettu. Older buildings with renovation loans (putkiremontti etc.) may rahastoida — **always verify with isännöitsijä**.

The tax advantage: for a sijoittaja, tuloutettu rahoitusvastike is fully deductible from rental income annually, reducing the tax bill. With a bank loan, only the interest (not principal) is deductible. This makes yhtiölaina more tax-efficient for investors (source: https://asuntosalkunrakentaja.fi/asuntosijoittaminen/rahoitusvastike-ja-yhtiolaina/).

### 5.3 New Regulation: 60% Debt Cap (2025)

Since July 2025, new construction yhtiölaina is capped at **60% of velaton hinta** (previously commonly 70–85%). This reduces the risk of extremely leveraged new builds (source: https://sijoitusasuntovahti.fi/taloyhtiolainojen-uudet-saannot/, Finanssivalvonta).

### 5.4 Yhtiölaina Risks for the €120k Cash Buyer

**For Category A purchases (minimal/no yhtiölaina):**
- Low risk. Monthly costs are just hoitovastike + utilities
- Full control over the asset — no third-party loan exposure

**For Category B purchases (low myyntihinta + heavy yhtiölaina):**
- **Misleading affordability:** Myyntihinta of €20–35k seems cheap, but velaton hinta may be €150–250k+
- **High monthly commitment:** Rahoitusvastike of €200–400/month on top of hoitovastike
- **Interest rate risk:** If taloyhtiö's loan terms reset at higher rates, rahoitusvastike can increase
- **Liquidity risk:** Difficult to sell — buyers must accept the large yhtiölaina burden
- **Developer risk:** Some uudiskohteet have struggled with vacancy and developer financial issues
- **Overvaluation risk:** New builds in outer Helsinki may have velaton hinnat that exceed market value

**Recommendation for this investor:** Given €120k cash budget and conservative profile, **strongly prefer Category A** (standard sales with minimal yhtiölaina in older buildings). The investor has the cash to buy outright, gaining the lowest possible monthly costs and full asset control. Category B only makes sense if the investor wants a newer, larger apartment and is comfortable with the ongoing cash flow commitment and risks.

### 9.1 Putkiremontti (Linjasaneeraus) Cost Benchmarks

| Source | Cost Metric | Helsinki/PKS Estimate |
|---|---|---|
| Sustera (Dec 2025) | Per apartment (avg traditional linjasaneeraus) | **~€50,000/apartment** |
| Sustera | €/m² range | **€500–1,500/m²** |
| UrakkaDiili (2026) | PKS estimate | **>€1,000/m²** |

(Sources: https://sustera.fi/ajankohtaista/uutiset/putkiremontin-hinta-kerrostalossa/, https://urakkadiili.fi/putkiremontti-hinta)

**Key insight from Sustera:** Cost is largely **per-bathroom, not per-m²**. A 25m² yksiö with 1 bathroom costs nearly the same as a 60m² apartment with 1 bathroom. For a yksiö, realistic putkiremontti cost: **€30,000–50,000** for a full traditional linjasaneeraus.

**What a putkiremontti includes:**
- Replacement of water supply and drainage pipes (vesi- ja viemäriputket)
- Often combined with: HVAC upgrade, electrical systems, wet room renovation
- Combining LVISA systems + märkätilat is 20% cheaper than separate renovations
- Duration: typically 2–5 months per line (linja), residents must vacate during work

### 9.2 Building Age Risk Assessment

| Building Era | Price Level | Putkiremontti Status | Key Risks | Key Benefits |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1930s–1950s** | Varies (Kallio, Käpylä premium) | Done or overdue | Structural issues, asbestos, very old systems | Character, central locations, high rental demand |
| **1960s–1970s** | Often cheapest | **IMMINENT or OVERDUE** — these are the danger zone | Full putkiremontti due, peltikylpyhuone = expensive, possibly julkisivuremontti too | Low purchase price already reflects risk, post-remontti value increases |
| **1980s–1990s** | Moderate | 10–20 years away | Pre-putkiremontti uncertainty, systems aging | Some time before major costs, often good layout |
| **2000s–2010s** | Higher | Not yet needed | Higher purchase price, possible yhtiölaina from build | Modern standards, low immediate renovation risk |
| **2020s (new build)** | Highest (velaton hinta) | Decades away | Heavy yhtiölaina, low market liquidity, developer risk | Brand new systems, energy efficient, low maintenance |

### 9.3 How to Assess Renovation Risk Before Purchase

**Must-check documents from isännöitsijäntodistus:**
1. **Kunnossapitotarveselvitys (PTS):** Lists all planned renovations for the next 5–10 years with cost estimates
2. **Taloyhtiön tilinpäätös:** Check reserve fund (korjausrahasto), current debts, and annual vastike income
3. **Putket ja viemärit:** When last inspected? Any kuntotutkimus (condition survey) results?
4. **Yhtiölaina status:** How much, what for, repayment timeline
5. **Energiatodistus:** Energy class impacts future costs and appeal

**Pricing principle:** The market typically prices in upcoming putkiremontti as a discount. A flat in a 1970s building with imminent putkiremontti will be priced **€20,000–40,000 below** similar flat in a post-remontti building. The investor should calculate: (purchase price + expected renovation share) < price of comparable post-remontti flat → good deal.

### 9.4 Putkiremontti Strategy for €120k Budget

**Option A: Buy post-remontti** (putkiremontti already completed)
- Higher purchase price (~€80k–120k for yksiö)
- Lower risk: new pipes, new bathroom, modern systems
- Lower ongoing costs, higher tenant appeal
- Less budget remaining for unexpected costs

**Option B: Buy pre-remontti at discount** (putkiremontti due within 5–10 years)
- Lower purchase price (~€50k–80k for yksiö)
- Must reserve €30k–50k for putkiremontti share
- Strategy: buy at €60k, reserve €50k for remontti, total ~€110k
- Risk: timing uncertain, may need to cover costs from savings
- Benefit: post-remontti value will rise significantly

**Recommended for 5–10 year horizon:** Option A (post-remontti) or early-phase pre-remontti (8–15 years out) to avoid renovation during the holding period. If child will eventually live there, a recently renovated building is preferable for comfort and reliability.

## Section 13: Purchase Process Checklist

### 13.1 Pre-Search Preparation

- [ ] Define search criteria: area (Myllypuro / alternatives), size (≥25m²), max velaton hinta (€75,000–120,000), building year range (prefer 1980s+ or post-remontti)
- [ ] Set up alerts on Oikotie.fi and Etuovi.com
- [ ] Contact 1–2 kiinteistönvälittäjä (real estate agents) active in target areas for off-market opportunities
- [ ] Open a savings account dedicated to the investment (separates funds)

### 13.2 Viewing & Due Diligence Checklist

**Before viewing — request from seller/agent:**
- [ ] **Isännöitsijäntodistus** (management certificate) — the single most important document:
  - Taloyhtiön yhtiölaina: total amount, share for this unit, repayment schedule
  - Vastike: hoitovastike, vesimaksu, rahoitusvastike (if any), pääomavastike
  - Vastike history: has it increased significantly?
  - Korjaushistoria: what renovations have been done?
  - **Kunnossapitotarveselvitys (PTS):** 5–10 year renovation plan — check for putkiremontti, julkisivuremontti, hissiremontti
  - Osakkeistuvat osuudet: autopaikka, varasto
  - Yhtiöjärjestys: any restrictions on renting?
- [ ] **Taloyhtiön tilinpäätös** (annual accounts): check revenue, expenses, reserve fund, debts
- [ ] **Energiatodistus** (energy certificate): energy class, heating costs
- [ ] **Pohjapiirros** (floor plan): room layout, wet room location

**During viewing — check physically:**
- [ ] Water pressure (run taps and flush toilet)
- [ ] Moisture damage (check bathroom corners, under sink, window sills)
- [ ] Ventilation (feel air flow at vents)
- [ ] Noise level (traffic, neighbors)
- [ ] Building common areas condition (stairwell, basement, piha)
- [ ] Parking/storage (kellarikoppi, polkupyörävarasto)

### 13.3 Making an Offer & Negotiation

- [ ] Check comparable recent sales (Asuntojenhinnat.fi, Etuovi hintaseuranta) to determine fair price
- [ ] In current market (2026, buyer's market): **offer 5–10% below asking price** — market has been declining, sellers are motivated
- [ ] If flat needs renovation or has upcoming taloyhtiö remontti, factor cost into offer
- [ ] Offer can be conditional on isännöitsijäntodistus review (though unusual in Finland)
- [ ] Negotiate inclusion of any movable property (irtaimisto: appliances, curtains, etc.)

### 13.4 Purchase Transaction

1. **Kauppakirja (deed of sale):** Signed by buyer and seller. In Finland, no notary required for asunto-osake transactions. Agent typically prepares the document.
2. **Payment:** Full purchase price transferred to seller's account at signing or via escrow agreed in kauppakirja
3. **Osakekirja / osuustodistus:** Physical share certificate transferred from seller to buyer (or updated in Huoneistotietojärjestelmä digital registry)

### 13.5 Post-Purchase Administrative Steps

- [ ] **Varainsiirtovero:** File via OmaVero and pay within 2 months of purchase (1.5% of kauppahinta)
- [ ] **Huoneistotietojärjestelmä registration:** Register ownership with Maanmittauslaitos (€89 fee). Now mandatory for all housing share transfers.
- [ ] **Notify isännöitsijä** of ownership change
- [ ] **Home insurance (kotivakuutus):** Take out landlord's insurance (vuokranantajan vakuutus, ~€100–200/year)
- [ ] **Renovate if needed:** Minor cosmetic renovation before renting (painting, floor touch-up)
- [ ] **Find tenant:** List on Vuokraovi.com, Oikotie vuokrat, Facebook marketplace. Or use vuokranvälityspalvelu (~1 month rent as fee)
- [ ] **Vuokrasopimus:** Sign rental agreement (use Vuokranantajat.fi or Kiinteistöliitto standard template)
- [ ] **Tax declaration:** Report rental income in spring tax return (veroilmoitus). Deduct eligible expenses.

## Executive Summary

### Helsinki Studio Flat Investment Analysis — Key Findings

**For: Private investor with €120,000 cash budget, 5–10 year rental horizon, future child housing**

Helsinki's apartment market has declined continuously for 3.5 years (the longest since 1947), with prices down 14% from peak. Both PTT and Hypo forecast a further 1.0–1.4% decline in 2026, with recovery expected from 2027 (+3% PKS). **This makes 2026 a near-optimal buying window** — prices are near cyclical bottom while transaction volumes are already rising.

Within the €120,000 budget, **25 Helsinki neighborhoods** offer studio flats ≥25m². After analyzing pricing, rental yield, transport, university proximity, building condition, and safety across all candidates, the analysis identifies:

**Top Pick: Myllypuro (00920)** — A post-putkiremontti yksiö (25–32m²) for €60,000–75,000 delivers a 5.3% after-tax yield, with €45,000–60,000 remaining in reserve. The neighborhood is adjacent to the Metropolia AMK campus, served by metro (15 min to center), and has undergone significant urban renewal. Structural student tenant demand ensures low vacancy.

**Runner-Up: Viikki (00790)** — A yksiö for ~€109,000 near the Helsingin yliopisto campus offers the lowest risk (modern 1990s–2010s buildings) with a reliable 3.2% after-tax yield and excellent safety. Best for "set and forget" investors who prioritize building quality over yield.

**Key numbers:**
- Transfer tax: 1.5% of kauppahinta (confirmed by 4 sources)
- Rental income tax: 30% on net income up to €30,000
- Average yksiö rent in Helsinki: €25/m²/month = €625 for 25m²
- Average hoitovastike: €5.00–5.22/m²/month (older buildings)
- Putkiremontti cost: ~€30,000–50,000 per yksiö (if needed)

**The single most important piece of advice:** Buy a post-putkiremontti building to avoid the largest risk to your investment. A 1980s building that has already completed linjasaneeraus offers the best combination of low price, manageable monthly costs, and decades of worry-free ownership. Combined with the exit strategy of having your child live there 2+ years before selling (triggering the tax-free own-residence exemption), this approach maximizes long-term financial benefit.

**Tax-free exit strategy:** Rent the flat for 5–10 years (collecting ~5% annual yield), then have your child move in as their primary residence for at least 2 continuous years. When they eventually sell, the entire capital gain is tax-free under Finland's own-residence exemption (oman asunnon verovapaus). This transforms the investment from a rental play into a multi-generational wealth transfer tool.
