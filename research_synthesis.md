## 2. EASA Regulatory Integration — Solo vs Enterprise Tiers

### 2.1 EASA Drone Categories Overview (EU Regulation 2019/947, 2019/945)

The European Union Aviation Safety Agency (EASA) defines **three operational categories:**

| Category | Risk Level | Authorization | Typical User |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Open** (A1/A2/A3) | Low | No authorization needed | Solo operators, hobbyists, basic commercial |
| **Specific** (STS/PDRA/LUC) | Medium | Declaration or Authorization required | Professional operators, enterprise |
| **Certified** | High | Full certification (like manned aviation) | Urban Air Mobility, large cargo drones |

Source: [EASA Open Category](https://www.easa.europa.eu/en/domains/drones-air-mobility/operating-drone/open-category-low-risk-civil-drones), [EASA Specific Category](https://www.easa.europa.eu/en/domains/drones-air-mobility/operating-drone/specific-category-civil-drones)

### 2.2 Open Category — Subcategories (Solo Operators)

| Subcategory | Description | Max Drone Weight | Training Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A1** | Fly over people (not assemblies) | C0 (<250g) or C1 (<900g) | A1/A3 online exam (free at NAA) |
| **A2** | Fly close to people | C2 (<4kg) | A1/A3 + A2 certificate (self-study + proctored exam) |
| **A3** | Fly far from people | C3/C4 (<25kg) | A1/A3 online exam |

**Key training facts:**
- A1/A3 competency certificate: **Online exam** administered by National Aviation Authority (NAA). Valid for **5 years**. Recognized across all EU + EEA states.
- A2 remote pilot certificate: **Additional self-study + supervised exam** at an NAA-recognized entity. Requires practical self-training declaration.
- No minimum flight hours mandated for Open category.

### 2.3 Specific Category — Enterprise Operations (STS, PDRA, SORA)

Four pathways to operate in the Specific category:

1. **STS Declaration (Standard Scenarios):** Pre-defined risk-assessed scenarios (STS-01: VLOS urban, STS-02: BVLOS rural). Requires STS remote pilot certificate from recognized training entity.
2. **PDRA (Pre-Defined Risk Assessment):** Operator applies for authorization using a simplified template.
3. **SORA (Specific Operations Risk Assessment):** Full risk assessment for novel/complex operations. Requires Operations Manual.
4. **LUC (Light UAS Operator Certificate):** Organization-level certification granting self-authorization privileges.

**STS Training requirements:**
- Mandatory theoretical knowledge training
- Practical skills training and assessment
- Provided by NAA-recognized Entities (training organizations)
- Valid across EU member states

**Enterprise must also:**
- Register as UAS operator
- Develop an Operations Manual
- Maintain insurance (mandatory in EU)
